    Charles Sturt University unveils first NSW TPG-aligned Climate Outlook for 2026

    Aligned with the NSW Treasury climate disclosure framework, this marks the University’s first formal climate-related financial disclosure. It sets a baseline for transparent reporting and future transition planning.

    What TPG24-33 Requires of CSU

    The four mandatory pillars — and what each means for a Phase 2 entity
    👥
    Governance
    Pillar 1
    MANDATORY:
    • Who oversees climate risk
    • How often they’re informed
    • How it links to strategy
    YEAR 1 RELIEF:

    Qualitative description accepted Year 1

    📈
    Strategy
    Pillar 2
    MANDATORY:
    • Physical & transition risks (S1–S6)
    • Business model effects
    • Financial position impacts (qualitative)
    YEAR 1 RELIEF:

    Scenario analysis NOT required Year 1

    🗄
    Risk Management
    Pillar 3
    MANDATORY:
    • How risks are identified & assessed
    • How risks are prioritised
    • Integration with enterprise risk
    YEAR 1 RELIEF:

    Simplified register approach acceptable

    🎯
    Metrics & Targets
    Pillar 4
    MANDATORY:
    • Scope 1 & 2 GHG emissions (mandatory)
    • Emission methodology disclosed
    • Progress against climate targets
    YEAR 1 RELIEF:

    Scope 3 EXCLUDED in Year 1
